<p><ul><li>Recombinant Coagulation Factor</li><li>Plasma Coagulation Factor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The blood coagulants market comprises two primary types: recombinant coagulation factors and plasma-derived coagulation factors. Recombinant coagulation factors are genetically engineered proteins used to treat bleeding disorders, offering reduced risk of disease transmission and consistency in product quality. In contrast, plasma-derived coagulation factors are derived from human plasma and are traditionally used for hemophilia treatment, providing a broader range of factor replacements but with potential risks associated with viral transmission. Both types are crucial in managing coagulation disorders effectively.</p></p>
