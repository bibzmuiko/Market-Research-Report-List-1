<p><p>Photoresist materials are essential in semiconductor lighting, enabling patterning on substrates during photolithography. The G-Line photoresist utilizes a wavelength of 436 nm, suitable for less advanced nodes. I-Line photoresist operates at 365 nm, allowing for finer features. KrF photoresist, with a wavelength of 248 nm, is commonly used for 90nm process nodes. ArF photoresist, at 193 nm, is prevalent in advanced technology nodes. EUV photoresist operates at 13.5 nm, supporting the cutting-edge 7nm and beyond processes, enhancing performance and efficiency.</p></p>
