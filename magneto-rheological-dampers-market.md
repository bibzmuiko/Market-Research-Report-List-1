<p><p>Magneto-Rheological (MR) dampers are pivotal in modern vibration control systems, featuring two primary market types: active dampers and semi-active dampers. Active dampers utilize an external energy source to adjust their damping characteristics dynamically, offering precise control over vibrations. In contrast, semi-active dampers employ MR fluids whose viscosity changes in response to magnetic fields, requiring less energy while adapting to varying conditions. Both types enhance performance in various applications, including automotive, aerospace, and civil engineering, ensuring superior stability and comfort.</p></p>
