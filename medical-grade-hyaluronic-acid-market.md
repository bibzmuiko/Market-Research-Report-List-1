<p><p>The medical-grade hyaluronic acid market is primarily categorized into two types: animal tissue-derived and microbial fermentation-derived. Animal tissue-derived hyaluronic acid is extracted from sources like rooster combs, offering high molecular weight and beneficial properties for medical applications. In contrast, microbial fermentation produces hyaluronic acid through bacterial processes, allowing for a more sustainable and controlled production method free from animal-derived materials. Both types serve critical roles in pharmaceuticals, dermal fillers, and wound healing, catering to diverse healthcare needs.</p></p>
